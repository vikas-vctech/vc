Islamic Finance Hub Roadmap — ME Government Organization

Case Study 03

Bank Entry into HK & China Merchant Acquisition Sector

Client Ask

Advise a leading bank on optimal modes of entry into Hong Kong and China's merchant acquisition sector.

Our Task

  • ▸Current payment landscape assessment — market economics, usage of cards/cash/checks, domestic payment schemes
  • ▸Emerging payment landscape — maturity spectrum of Payment Service Providers (PSP), contactless payments, OEM and proprietary wallets
  • ▸Payments value chain analysis — profiles of various types of merchant acquirers including network strength, POS terminal presence, e-commerce, 3/4 party models
  • ▸Key recent initiatives by leading players and identification of whitespace in the merchant acquisition market

Objectives Achieved

Recommended Hong Kong as an attractive market to enter for merchant acquisition owing to its relatively nascent market, lower competitive intensity, and strong regulatory environment for fintech innovation.

Case Study 06

Sports Medicine Centre Location & Feasibility — ME Health Authority

Client Ask

Location assessment and feasibility study for a Middle Eastern-based health authority looking to establish sports medicine centre(s) in Europe.

Our Task

  • ▸Divided the study into three stages to identify the right country using a selection-rejection criteria covering both quantitative and qualitative analysis
  • ▸Conducted 20+ expert interviews with sports medicine SMEs alongside extensive secondary research across five markets
  • ▸Five leading European markets assessed: Spain, UK, Germany, France, and Netherlands
  • ▸Analyzed: sports economy & infrastructure, sports medicine industry, financial investment climate, demand-supply gap, regulatory benefits, workforce availability, and ease of doing business
  • ▸Final market recommendation with financial feasibility and phased implementation roadmap

Objectives Achieved

The client appreciated the highly methodical approach and is at the preliminary stage of establishing their sports medicine centre in the recommended European market.
